Secret on the island

Synopsis

On an isolated island, there is a massive mansion, built by a painter who mysteriously disappeared over 20 years ago. An art appraiser is hired to spend a few days inside this mansion, searching for valuable paintings within it. To do this, he decides to bring his family on the trip. After all, what could possibly go wrong?

Spoilers about the season

Main events

The arrival of the Florences on the island is marked by encounters with Cavalcante Bueno and Celestino, the lighthouse keeper. They head to the locked mansion, discovering an old and elegant environment with clues about secrets inside. The next morning, at Barbara's house, Ela is confronted by her mother about the mansion, resulting in a quarrel. In the mansion, the Florence family explores the place, finding a mysterious painting that disturbs Amelie. Later, the Florences prepare for the beach. Milo, playing the banjo at home, joins the others, leading to an investigation into a photo of his mother. The situation intensifies when Angelina is attacked by a wild boar and wild dogs, saved by Bartô Castello. Milo is bitten by a venomous snake, but with everyone's help, he has no issues. After stabilizing Milo, they participate in a lively dinner in the village, where the Lighthouse Keeper appears, talking about Celestino.

On the Island of Tipora, the Florences investigate the mansion, unraveling clues about Constantino and Leila Lima. Wanderley, a new character, arrives on the island. Upon returning to the mansion, they discover notes related to keys and painting lessons. Barnabé, Adrian, and the police search for Amora, interrupting the investigation. Groups form to look for Amora, leading to the discovery of the deceased Cavalcante and a terrifying painting on the beach. The night is filled with nightmares for Wanderley, Amelie, Barbara, and Olivier, revealing supernatural visions and frightening creatures. As they share their experiences in the morning, they realize the influence of the painting and the lighthouse on their dreams. Each one faces unique challenges, such as liquid creatures, the Bride, and terrifying visions.

The morning brings impactful revelations for the Florences, while Wanderley and his group explore the church, facing supernatural conflicts. At the rally, Bartô is accused, resulting in confusion and a potential outbreak. Discoveries of autopsy papers and attempts to open locked drawers deepen the mysteries. The journey leads to drawings of Amora in Barnabé's house, connecting the characters. Everyone gathers in the forest, confronting the painting and the hidden suitcase. The atmosphere becomes unsettling, and after a long night, they decide to eat and rest, concluding another tumultuous day in Tipora. Tension rises with Bartô's death and the revelation of a macabre painting, indicating dark mysteries on the island.

On the last night in Tipora, Olivier has intense visions while playing Noralma's painting on the piano. Milo spiritually merges with his brother, Miguel, understanding the nature of the Imaginary Friend. While exploring the third floor, they discover a room with a wheelchair, a musket, and a revealing painting. In visions, everyone shares past experiences and makes choices at doors, unlocking a key on the piano. Using the key in the studio, they discover a painting connected to the past, revealing the true art. Cavalcante is found near death, injected by Barnabé, revealing a pact and everyone's involvement on the island. With Cavalcante's suitcase, they find vials, a diary, and a scanner. The story unfolds, and Constantino's painting reveals his older face. They find Otávio, now zombified, and uncover Barnabé's disturbances. The night culminates in a quest for truth, unraveling secrets, and facing chaos in Tipora.

Barnabé, a master manipulator, executed a plan to instill fear, creating the creature. In the final confrontation, the monstrosity devours faces, leading to impactful deaths: Amora, Bárbara, Wanderley, and Milo fall victim to the creature. The group, determined for revenge, attacks the beast with resolve. Amelie uses a final ritual, electrocuting the creature, ultimately defeating it. The losses are painful, but they manage to save part of the island. The Florence family reunites with their mother, and together, they face the disturbed Montel. At the docks, agents from the Ordo Realitas arrive, offering help and inviting the Florence siblings to join the Order. Arthur Cervero and Walter, the agents, depart to investigate the island. The Florence family and the survivors leave the island of Tipora, concluding a chapter marked by secrets, tragedies, and the paranormal intervention of the Ordo Realitas.
